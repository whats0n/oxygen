A deal is defined as an event which has been both registered by the matching (clearing) and the cash flow is triggered by the smart contract.

Type and amount of borrowed ERC20 tokens

  • Deal duration
  • Type of tokens used as a collateral

The lender must specify:

  • Asset and size to be offered to borrowers
  • Collateral requirements
  • Borrower's minimum credit rating
  • Contract maturity
  • Interest rate
  • Allow pooled offers
  • Proposal expiration

The borrower must specify:

  • Asset and quantity seeked
  • Contract maturity
  • Collateral he is ready to post
  • Proposal expiration time

The matching engine presents various books and maturities where users can either take or provide liquidity (market & limit orders). The auction is organized in the same fashion as the CME interest rate derivatives section.

The risk management toolkit provides suggestions, ceiling, floors and indicative rates for offer submission based on market events.

A set of web applications where participants interact with decentralized REPO contracts.

The core of decentralized REPO transaction. Smart contracts can be directly used by any party within Ethereum network. The smart contract acts as the settlement mechanism for deals that occurred through the public auction.

The rating rewards and penalizes users proportionally to the deal’s volumes. A separate smart contract stores the rating data.

A treasury management streamlining feature. The lender can specify that he wants the process of realization to be automatically performed through Etherdelta. In such event, orders are automatically placed and the deal’s proceeds are directly transferred to the the lender.

The system fee is paid in LendOS tokens. The fee automatically includes the settlement fee (gas required to interact with the smart contract)

Loan conditions are defined as an interest rate from notional - however the actual cash flow is referenced from the collateral, not the borrowed asset.

Any ERC20-compatible tokens are acceptable as collateral (subject to market’s acceptance). The collateral is held by the smart contract which then delivers it to either party depending on the credit-event.

If the borrower fails to return borrowed tokens when the deal has expired, the deal is automatically considered default. The lender will then be able to claim the collateral from the contract.

When the borrower successfully returns borrowed tokens with interests while the deal is still active (not expired), the deal is automatically considered complete.

In case of a successfully closed loan the rating increases

The borrower rating is updated according to the outcome of the deal.

  • In case of completed deal, it goes up.
  • Rating change is proportionate to the volume of the deal.
  • Rating of all participants is stored in a separate contract.

Optional. If the lender has specified this clause in the contract automated realization will take place should a default occur.

Optional. If the lender has specified this clause in the contract the interest is paid from the collateral.

Interest is an agreed percentage of the collateral assets. The lender claims it when the deal is closed gracefully.

All borrowing operations are secured and the lender will always receive the posted collateral if the borrower decides to default on payment. The system is designed to provide valuations which take into account such events and turn them into potential profits.

The original tokens, used by the borrower in accordance with the deal, are returned to the lender using the ERC20 contract interface.

When the deal is closed gracefully, the collateral is unblocked from the REPO contract and is returned to the borrower. Interests are payed to the lender.
